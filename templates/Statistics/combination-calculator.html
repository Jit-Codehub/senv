What is meant by the Combination?

In mathematics, combination in simple words says that the no.of ways you can choose p elements out of a set containing n possible combinations without repetition and with repetition. And for combinations order is not important. For example, in a bag we have 10 balls, where each one is of different color. you have picked five balls at random. we need to find how many different and same combinations we get.

Combination Formula

The following combination formula that will allow you to define the number of combinations. Let's see below.

Combination with repetition

C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!)

Combination without repetition

C(n,r) = (n+r-1)!/r!(n-1)!

where,

C(n,r) is the number of combinations.

n is the total no.of elements in the given set.

r is the no.of outcomes.

How to Compute Number of Possible Combinations?

Follow the guidelines carefully to calculate the combinations to get possible outcomes.

  • Primary thing you need to do is note down the values given in the problem.
  • Then, apply the formula of combinations (with or without repetition).
  • And, substitute the given values in the formula.
  • Finally, you will get the possible outcomes.

Finding Combinations Examples

Question 1: A bag filled with fifteen pebbles, where each one is of different color. You pick 4 pebbles at random. How many different combinations you can get?

Solution:

Given, C(15,4)

Apply the formula of combination without repetition

C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!)

C(15,4)= 15!/(4!(15-4)!)

= 15!/(4!x 11!)

= 1365

Apply the formula of combination with repetition

C(n,r) = (n+r-1)!/r!(n-1)!

C(15,4)= (15+4-1)!/4!(4-1)!

= 18!/(4! x 3!)

= 3060

Thus, possible outcomes of combination with repetition is 3060 and without repetition is 1365

FAQs on Combinations

1. How to calculate the possible combinations?

In combinations we have two types. One is with repetition and another one is without repetition. We calculate the possible combinations by using the formulas. For Combination without repetition C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!), and for with repetition C(n,r) = (n+r-1)!/r!(n-1)!

2. What is Combinations?

Arrangement of objects in which the order does not matter and gives the possible outcomes with and without repetition is called combination.

3. What is value of 2C3 without repetition?

C(3,2) = 3!/ 2!(3-2)! = 3!/2!(1!) = 3
