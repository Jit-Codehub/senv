What do you mean by Linear Correlation Coefficient?

In statistics, linear correlation coefficient is similarly known as pearson's correlation coefficient. Linear correlation coefficient is nothing but the linear relationship between two variables. And it is expressed as 'r', the value of r ranges from -1 to +1. Here, the positive number shows the positive relationship and the negative value shows the negative relationship.

How do you find the Linear Correlation Coefficient?

Follow below guidelines to solve the linear correlation coefficient very easily by hand.

  • Look for the given values in the problem.
  • Then, the Linear Correlation coefficient formula is n∑XY - (∑X)(∑Y) / Sqrt([n∑X2 - (∑X)2][n∑Y2 - (∑Y)2])
  • Find the values ∑x, ∑y, ∑xy, and ∑x2, ∑y2.
  • Substitute the values and simplify it.
  • At last, you will get the desired answer.
