How do you Calculate the Probability of a Dice?

Before finding the probability of a dice we need to make sure for what kind of dice we are about to find the probability. Before diving into the topic let us see few variables used in the formulas

  • n - the number of dice
  • s - the number of a individual die faces
  • p - the probability of rolling any value from a die
  • P - the overall probability

There is a simple relationship - p = 1/s

Probability of Rolling Same Value on each Die: Since the chance of getting a particular value on a single die is p we simply need to multiply the probability by itself as many times the number of dice. In other words, we can put is as P = pn

Probability of Rolling all Values Equal or Higher than y: This scenario is similar to the above one but the only difference is this time p is 1/s multiplied by all the possible conditions satisfying the initial condition.

Probability of Rolling all Values Equal or Lower than y: This case is also similar to the earlier one but it's just that here we are finding the numbers which are equal or lower than our target.

The probability of rolling exactly X same values (equal to y) out of the set: This case is a bit different to the earlier ones. But Binomial Probability comes in handy here. The formula for determining the binomial probability is P(X=r) = nCr * pʳ * (1-p)ⁿ⁻ʳ where r is the number of successes, nCr is the number of combinations.

The probability of rolling at least X same values (equal to y) out of the set: In this particular scenario the probability is the sum of probabilities.

The probability of rolling an exact sum r out of the set of n s-sided dice: The general formula is quite complex.

The probability of rolling a sum out of the set, not lower than X: Similar to the earlier cases we need to determine the results that match the initial condition and then divide them with number of all possibilities.

The probability of rolling a sum out of the set, not higher than X: The process is almost the same but here we need to add only the sums below or equal to target. To find sum of all the sums can take up huge time so you can go with the complementary events as total probability sums to 1 you can simply subtract from 1.

Dice Probability Questions and Answers

Question 1: A die is rolled. What is the probability of getting an odd number?

Solution:

We know the formula to determine probability = No. of favorable outcomes/Total No. of possible outcomes

Favorable outcomes for getting an odd number when a dice is rolled is(1, 3, 5) 3

Total number of possible outcomes = 6

Substituting the given values we have the probability of getting an odd number = 3/6

Probability of getting an odd number = 1/2

Therefore, probability of getting an odd number when a dice is rolled is 1/2.

Question 2: Amar throws two normal, six-faced dice. How much chance does he have for a total of at least 10?

Solution:

If 2 dice are rolled the possible combinations are (6*6) i.e. 36

No. of favorable outcomes of getting a total of atleast 10 are 6: 4+6, 6+4, 5+5, 5+6, 6+5, 6+6

Probability of obtaining atleast 10 when 2 dice are rolled = No. of favorable outcomes/ Total no. of possible outcomes

Probability of obtaining atleast 10 when 2 dice are rolled = 6/36

Probability of obtaining atleast 10 when 2 dice are rolled = 1/6

Therefore, Amar has a probability of 1/6 to get a total of atleast 10.
