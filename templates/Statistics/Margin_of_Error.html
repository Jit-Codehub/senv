What is Margin of Error Mean?

In statistics, margin of error is also known as confidence interval, and it is a measurement of error using sample population, z-score and sample size. Let us see the below formula for Margin of error.

Formula for Margin of Error

Margin of Error = z * √p * (1 - p) / √n

MOE (with finite population ) = z * √p * (1 - p) / √(N - 1) * n / (N - n)

Where,

Z is the z-score, will be taken from the table.

p is the sample proportion.

n is sample size.

N is the population size.

Sample Example for Margin of Error

Example

Question: A store conducted a survey to identify how many laptop users use their company laptops. They surveyed 1500 laptop users and found that 520 people use their brand.

Solution

Given, z = 1.96, p = 0.5, n = 1500

As we know that at 95% confidence level Z-score value is 1.96.

Margin of Error (MOE) formula,

MOE = z * √p * (1 - p) / √n

MOE = 1.96 * √0.5 * (1 - 0.5) / √1500

MOE = 0.98 / 38.73 * 100 = 2.53%

Thus, the margin of error is ±2.53%.
