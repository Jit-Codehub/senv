What is Mean?

Mean is defined as the average of the given numbers. In statistics, the mean is one of the measure of central tendency. All three measures mean, median and mode define the central value of the observations. Mean denotes the equal distribution of values for the given data set and it is the unique value that represents collected data.

Usually, the symbol of mean is x̄ or μ.

The mean formula is given here.

x̄ = (x₁ + x₂ + x3 +….+xn)/n

Where,

n is the total number of observations

How to Find the Mean or Average?

In order to find the mean of list of numbers esaily, you should know the process to calculate it. So, to master your skills, we have given the step by step process with solved example.

  • Firstly, take the set of numbers.
  • Find the sum of all numbers in the given set.
  • Thereafter, divide the sum by the set of numbers count to know mean or average value.

Example:

Calculate the arithmetic mean of 15,12,10,8,6,20,23,18,25,36.

Solution:

Given set of numbers are 15,12,10,8,6,20,23,18,25,36

The total number of observations are 10

Sum of observations in set = 15+12+10+8+6+20+23+18+25+36

= 173

Mean x̄ = (x₁ + x₂ + x3 +….+xn)/n = ∑x/n

= (15+12+10+8+6+20+23+18+25+36)/10

= 173/10

= 17.3

Therefore, average or mean of 15,12,10,8,6,20,23,18,25,36 is 17.3.

1. What are the types of mean?

The three different types of mean in statistics are arithmetic mean, geometric mean and harmonic mean.
