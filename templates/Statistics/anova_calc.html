What is Meant by ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)?

In statistics, ANOVA test is a one way analysis of variance test. It is used to determine the significant difference between the means of groups. This method is similar to two sample t-test. It checks the impact of many factors by comparing the means of various samples.

The formulas to calculate ANOVA are given here:

  • SSb=∑i=1 to k ni(xi-x)2, where ni is the number of subjects in the ith group
  • SSw=∑i=1 to k (ni-1)Si2, where Si is the standard deviation of the ith group
  • Total sum of squares SSt=SSb+SSw
  • Mean square between groups MSb=SSb/(K-1)
  • Mean square within groups MSw=SSw/(N-k)
  • F-Statistic or F-ratio F=MSb/MSw

Steps to Calculate ANOVA

The below provided step by step procedure is useful for you while solving the One way anova. Use these steps and get the result easily.

  • Take any number of groups with data values.
  • Find the mean and standard deviation for the groups.
  • Calculate sum of squares between groups and sum of squares within groups.
  • Substitute your values in the formulae and do mathematical calculations.

Example

Question: Solve the one way anova of the following groups Group 1={1,2,3,4,5}, Group 2={5,1,7,8,6}, Group 3={0,1,4,6,3}?

Solution:

Given data is Group 1={1,2,3,4,5}, Group 2={5,1,7,8,6}, Group 3={0,1,4,6,3}

Mean of Group 1=1+2+3+4+5/5=3

Mean of Group 2=5+1+7+8+6/5=27/5=5.4

Mean of Group 3=0+1+4+6+3/5=14/5=2.8

SD of Group 1=1.5811

SD of Group 2=2.7019

Standard Deviation of Group 3=2.3875

Total Number of subjects in each group (N)=5

SSb=∑i=1 to k ni(xi-x)2

SSb=12

SSw==∑i=1 to k (ni-1)Si2

=62.0012

SSt=SSb+SSw = 12+62.0012=74.0012

MSb=SSb/(K-1)=12/(3-1)

=6

MSw=SSw/(N-k)

=62.0012/(15-3)= 5.1668

F ratio=MSb/MSw

=1.1613

FAQ's on One Way Analysis of Variance Calculator

1. How do I calculate ANOVA?

2. What is ANOVA in simple terms?

ANOVA is a one way of analysis of variance test. It is used to find the significant difference between the means of three or more groups.

3. What are the disadvantages of ANOVA?

The disadvantage of ANOVA is it is difficult to analyze ANOVA under strict assumptions regarding the nature of data. It is not helpful to compare with the t-test that there is no special interpretation of the significance of two means.

4. What are the types of tests in Anova?

The two types of tests in Anova are One way anova and two way anova with replication or without replication.
