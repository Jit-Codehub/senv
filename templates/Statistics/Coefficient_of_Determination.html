What is the Coefficient of Determination?

R-squared is the coefficient of determination which shows the relation between dependent and independent variables. Coeffecient of determination is also known as R-Squared and it is denoted by R2.

Formula of R-squared (or) Coefficient of Determination

Let us see the formula of R-squared (or) Coefficient of Determination

R-squared (r2) = Σ(xi - x̅) (yi - ȳ) / Σ(xi - x̅)2 (yi - ȳ)2

Where,

r2 = Coefficient of Determination

r = Correlation Coefficient

xi = Element of x

yi = Element of y

x̅ = Mean of x

ȳ = Mean of y

Coefficient of Determination Example

Example

Question: Find the Coefficient of Determination by following data:

X = 5, 10, 12 , Y = 3, 6, 9

Solution:

Given data is X = 5, 10, 12, Y = 3, 6, 9, total elements = 3

Using the R- squared formula

r2 = Σ(xi - x̅) (yi - ȳ) / Σ(xi - x̅)2 (yi - ȳ)2

Substitute the values in the formula,

Mean of X= 9

Mean of Y= 6

r2 = [(5-9)(3-6)+(10-9)(6-6)+(12-9)(9-6)]/[(5-9)2(3-6)2+(10-9)2(6-6)2+(12-9)2(9-6)2]

r2 = [(-4)(-3)+(1)(0)+(3)(3)]/[(-4)2(-3)2+(1)2(0)2+(3)(3)]

r2 = [(12)+1+9]/[(16)(9)+(1)+(9)(9)]

r2 = [22]/[144+1+81]

r2 = [22]/[226] = 0.0973

Thus, Coefficient of Determination (r2) = 0.0973
