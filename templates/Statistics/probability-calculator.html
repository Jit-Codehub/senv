What is Meant by Probability?

In Statistics, probability states that the ratio of no.of favorable outcomes to the total number of outcomes of an event. probability is the number always lies between 0 and 1. And in this 1 is considered to be possibility(true) and 0 is considered to be impossibility (false). we will use the probability to predict the outcomes.

Formula for Probability

P(E) = n(E) / n(S)

Probability Formulas With Two Events

Let us see the formulas with two events to get the output.

1. A∩B consists of set of elements that are common in A and B.

Formula: P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)

2. A∪B consists of the set of elements that are present in A and B.

Formula:P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B)

3. A∆B is the symmetric difference between AUB and A∩B.

Formula:P(A∆B) = (A U B) – (A ∩ B)

4. (A∪B)' is the intersection of complement of A and complement of B.

Formula: P(((A∪B)') = A'∩ B'

5. A' is defined as the set of elements that is available in A and not in B.

Formula: A'= 1-P(A)

6. B' is defined as the set of elements that is available in B and not in A.

Formula: B'= 1-P(B)

Question 1: Find the probability of the events for P(A)=2, P(B) = 5?

Solution

Given, P(A)=2, P(B) = 5

Formulas of Multiple Events.

P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)

= 2 x 5 = 10

P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B)

= 2 + 5 - 10 = -3

P(A∆B) = (A U B) – (A ∩ B)

= -3+10 = 7

A'= 1-P(A)

= 1-2 =-1

B'= 1-P(B)

= 1-5 =-4
