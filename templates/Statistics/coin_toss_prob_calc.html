How to find Coin Toss Probability?

Probability is nothing but the ratio of favorable outcomes to entire number of outcomes. This formula applies to coin toss probability too. Since the number of outcomes of coin toss are independent, total number of outcomes are equal to total number of trails times the number of possible outcomes in a single trial i.e. 2 and number of favorable outcomes is equal to number of possible combinations.

No.of Favorable Outcomes = nCh/2n

Probability = Number of favorable outcomes/Total number of outcomes

In the above formula n is the total no. of trails and h is the number of heads in the number of trails.


Solved Examples on finding Coin Toss Probability

Question 1: What will be the probability of getting 3 heads in 5 coin tosses?

Solution:

Given Number of trials (n) = 3

Total possible outcomes = 2n = 2 5 = 32

Number of heads (h) = 3

We know the formula to find favorable outcomes is nCh/2n

Substituting the given inputs in the above formula we have coin toss probability = 5C3 = 10

Now probability = 10/32

Probability = 0.3125

Therefore probability of geeting 3 heads in 5 coin tosses is 0.3125

Question 2: On tossing a coin thrice, what is the probability of getting exactly only one head?

Solution:

On tossing a coin thrice, the possible outcomes are {HHH, HHT, HTT, TTT, THH, HTH, TTH, THT}

Therefore, the total number of outcomes is 8

Getting only one head includes {HTT, TTH, THT}

Therefore, the number of favorable outcomes is 3

Hence, using the coin toss probability formula, the probability of getting exactly one head is 3/8
