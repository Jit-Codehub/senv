What is Meant by Chi-Square in Statistics?

In statistics, a chi-square is a test that assesses how a model compares to actual observed data. Also, it is the difference between the observed and expected frequencies of the results of a set of events or variables. The symbol of chi-square is 'χ2' and it depends on the size of the difference between actual and observed values, the degrees of freedom, and the sample size.

For calculating a chi-square statistic, the data utilized should be random, raw, taken from independent variables, adapted from a large enough sample, mutually exclusive. The two main kinds of chi-square tests are as follows:

  • The test of independence
  • The goodness of fit test

Chi-Square Test Formula

Before we go for the chi-square formula, let's learn the representation of the chi-square. It is denoted by χ2.

The formula for the Chi-Square Test is given as:

χ2 = ∑(Oi – Ei)2/Ei

Where,

Oi: Observed frequency
Ei: Expected frequency
∑: Summation
χ2: Chi Square Value

How To Calculate the Chi Square By Hand?

Let's see the step-wise approach of calculating the value of chi-square from the listed points:

  • First and foremost, take off (subtract) expected frequency from the related observed frequency ie., (O-E)
  • Now, continue step-1 by squaring each obtained value i.e,(O-E)^2.
  • Divide all the values in step 2 by the expected frequencies i.e., (O-E)^2/E.
  • Do a summation of the obtained values in step 3 to find the chi-square value.
  • Finally, we have to compare the calculated chi-square value with the critical chi-square value.

Look at the below-solved example on how to find the chi-square test value manually and get familiar with the concept.

Example:

Based on the survey on cars owned by each family in the locality the data has been arranged in the following table.

Number of Cars Oi Ei
One car 20 15.6
Two cars 25 20
Three cars 5 7
Total 50

Solution:

Let's set up the following table like this:

Number of Cars Oi Ei (Oi – Ei)2 (Oi – Ei)2/Ei
One car 20 15.6 19.36 1.24
Two cars 25 20 25 1.25
Three cars 5 7 4 0.57
Total 50     3.06

Hence, χ2= ∑(Oi – Ei)2/Ei = 3.06

FAQs on Free & Handy Chi Square Online Calculator

1. What is the chi-square probability?

The chi-square or χ2-distribution or chi-squared distribution with k degrees of freedom in probability theory and statistics is the distribution of a sum of the squares of k independent standard normal random variables.

3. What is a chi-square used for?

The chi-square statistical test is utilized for comparing observed outcomes with expected outcomes. The main goal of this test is to find if a difference between observed data & expected data is because of chance, or if it is as a result of a relationship between the variables you are studying.

4. What is a chi-square p-value?

The probability of getting a chi-square as big or more than that in the current experiment is called p-value in a chi-square analysis.
