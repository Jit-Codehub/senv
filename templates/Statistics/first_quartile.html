What does First Quartile (Q1) or Lower Quartile Mean?

The first quartile (Q1) is the lower quartile that lies between the first term and the median of the given set of observations. It is 25% of the sample data.

First Quartile Q1 or Lower Quartile Formula

How to Find the Lower Half or First Quartile?

The detailed process to calculate the first quartile of the data set is provided here. So, follow these simple steps and understand how to find lower quartile of the numbers manually.

  1. Firstly, arrange the data set numbers in ascending order.
  2. Make a note of the length of the data set.
  3. The data should be divided into two halves.
  4. Take the first half of the data to find Q1.
  5. Determine the median of the first quartile of the data.
  6. The median of the data set is the lower quartile value for the given set of data.

FAQs on Lower or 1st Quartile Calculator

1. How do you find the 1st quartile?

To find the 1st quartile value for the given data set, you have to enter the data set in the input fields of the calculator and press the calculate button.

2. Is the 1st quartile 25%?

Yes, the sample 25 percentile is called the first quartile. And the sample 50% is called sample median or 2nd quartile and 75% is called the third quartile.

3. What are Q1 Q2 and Q3?

Q1 is the first quartile or lower quartile, which lies between the first term and median of the data set. Q2 is the second quartile or median of the data set. Q3 is the middle value of the median and last term and it is called as upper quartile or third quartile.

4. Is the first quartile the lower quartile?

Yes, the 1st quartile Q1 is also known as the lower quartile. It can be defined as the middle number between the smallest number of the data set.
