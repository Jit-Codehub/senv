What is Shannon Entropy (or) Information Entropy?

In Statistics, entropy is a rate of uncertainty combined with random variables. Shannon Entropy permits to evaluate the average minimum number of bits required to encode a string based on the frequency. Shannon entropy is important part of information theory. Entropy is denoted by the letter 'H(x)'.

Shannon Entropy Formula:

H(x)= Σnn-1 p(xi) logb 1/p(xi)

p(xi) is a single probability event.

n is the number of elements.
