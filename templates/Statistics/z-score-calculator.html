What is Meant by Z Score & Its Formulas?

The definition of z score is as the distance between the mean score and the trail data points states in terms of standard deviation. The value of the z-score is utilized to explain the normal distribution.

The other names of z score in statistical data analysis are standard score, z value, normal score, and standardized score. For more information such as formula and process to calculate z score, step into our further modules.

Z Score Formula

1. The standard z score formula for a sample is: z = (x – μ) / σ

For instance, consider that you have a test score of 200. The test has a mean (μ) of 100 and a standard deviation (σ) of 5. By taking a normal distribution, the z score would be:

z = (x – μ) / σ
= (200 – 100) / 5 = 100/5 = 20.

The z score means how many standard deviations from the mean your score. Here, your score is 20 standard deviations above the mean.

2. The formula to calculate the z-score of a sample with a known population mean and standard deviation.

z = (xmean - μ) / σ/√n

Where

z is the z score value

x is the raw data point

xmean is the sample data set mean

n is the sample data set size

μ is the population mean

σ is the population standard deviation

How to Find Z Score Manually?

Follow the below simple steps and find z values manually with ease. Here are a few different ways to calculate the z score value. You can pick any of the methods as per the information you have in the given problem. So, check out the methods without any fail and get used to calculating the standard scores manually.

Finding Z Score By Data Points

  • Firstly, note down the data points, the population mean, population standard deviation from the given question.
  • Now, take the population mean and subtract the data points from it.
  • At last, divide the outcome by population SD to attain the z-score.

Calculating Z Value using Sample Mean and Size

  • After getting all the required inputs from the given question we have to continue with the next step.
  • Subtract the sample mean from the population mean & multiply it with the square root of data size.
  • Once you have calculated the result of the above step, divide the result by the population standard deviation to get the output of the z score.

Steps to Find Standard Score using Data Sample

  • Lets' consider the input values to calculate the z score.
  • At first, find the mean for the given data sample.
  • Obtain the difference between the population mean and data sample mean.
  • Do multiplication with the square root of data sample size.
  • Finally, perform division for the result and population standard deviation to get the result of the z score.

Example:

During the exam, five kids scored 45, 50, 55, 62, and 75 points. Find the z score of the result 62?

Solution:

Given points are 45, 50, 55, 62, and 75

Now, find the mean of the points μ = (45+50+55+62+75) = 287/5 = 57.4

Now, find the individal values of (x - μ)² for each result:

(45-57.4)² = 153.76
(50-57.4)² = 54.76
(55-57.4)² = 5.76
(62-57.4)² = 21.16
(75-57.4)² = 309.76

Calculate the SD of the data points (σ)

σ = √[(153.76+54.76+5.76+21.16+309.76)/5]
σ = √[ 545.2/5]
σ = √109.04
σ = 10.4422

Substitute these values in z score formula for x = 62

z = (62 - 57.4) / 10.4422
z = 0.44

Hence, the z score value of the result 62 is 0.44.
