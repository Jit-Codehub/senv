What is Relative Risk?

Relative risk is defined as the ratio of the probability of the disease exposed and the probability of the disease unexposed. Relative risk is also called as the risk ratio. And we calculate the risk ratio by using the formula. Let us see the formula below.

Relative Risk (RR) = (probability of disease exposed) / (probability of disease unexposed) = [A/(A+B)] / [C/(C+D)]

Here, there is an interpretation.

If the risk ratio = 1, then there is no association

If the risk ratio < 1, then the association is negative

If the risk ratio > 1, then the association is positive

How do you Calculate the Relative Risk?

Here is the step by step procedure to calculate the relative risk in easy process.

  • Write down the values which is given in the problem.
  • Then, apply the formula of relative risk and substitute the values.
  • Finally, you will get the answer of risk ratio.

Relative Risk Examples

Question: Calculate the relative risk using the data.

Disease No Disease
Exposed 3 100
NotExposed 127 280

Solution

Given, a = 3, b = 100, c = 127, d = 280

Formula for relative risk

RR = [A/(A+B)] / [C/(C+D)]

Here, standard error of confidence level is 95%

Substitute the values

= [3/(3+100)] / [127/(127+280)]

= 0.0291 / 0.312

= 0.0933

Thus the relative risk is less than 1, the association is negative.
