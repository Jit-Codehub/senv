What is Meant by Binomial Distribution & Formula?

The binomial distribution is a general discrete distribution utilized in statistics, as it summarizes the number of trails, or observations while every trial has the same probability of obtaining one specific value.

For example, if we have determined the outcome out of two is outlined as a success. Then, the probability of x successes out of N trails can be calculated as:

P(X) = nCx * px * (1 – p)(n-x)

or

P(X) = nCx px qn-x

Where,

n = Total number of trials
x = Total number of successful trials
p = probability of success in a single trial
q = probability of failure in a single trial = 1-p

Criteria of Binomial Distribution

There are three distinct criteria of binomial distributions. Here we have computed those three binomial distribution criteria that need to satisfy:

  • The number of experiments or trials should be fixed. As you can only find out the probable occurrence chance of success in a trial and also must have a finite number of trials.
  • Each trial is independent. Not any one of your trials must affect the possibility of the next trial.
  • The probability constantly stays the same and equal. The probability of winning may be equivalent to more than one trial.

It is mandatory to satisfy all these three criteria before performing any experiment to find the distribution is binomial or not. After figuring out them, you can easily apply the binomial formula and find out the probability distribution.

Binomial Distribution Examples

Check out the below worked examples on how to find the binomial distribution manually and improve your problem-solving skills.

Example:

A coin is tossed 5 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 3 tails in these 5 tosses?

Solution:

Given Number of trails = 5

Number of successes = 3

Probability of getting tails in a single coin toss = 0.5

Apply the binomial probability distribution formula ie.,

b(r: n, p) = (n/r) pr (1-p)n-r or nCr * pr * (1-p)n-r

Substitute the given values in the above formula

b(r: n,p) = 5C3 x (0.5)3 x (1-0.5)5-3
= 5C3 x (0.5)3 x (0.5)2
= 0.313

Hence, the probability of getting exactly 3 tails in these 5 tosses is 0.313.
