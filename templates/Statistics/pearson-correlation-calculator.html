What is Pearson Correlation Coefficient?

In statistics, correlation defines the relationship between two variables. The correlation coefficient is the measure of the correlation. The value of the Pearson correlation coefficient product is between -1 and +1. The possibilities of the correlation coefficient are given here.

  • If the correlation coefficient is 0, then the data has no correlation.
  • When the value of the correlation coefficient is +1, then the data are positively correlated.
  • If the correlation coefficient value is -1, then the data has a negative correlation.

Steps to Calculate Pearson Correlation

Get to know the step by step process on how to find the Pearson correlation coefficient by referring the following sections.

  1. Take two data sets i.e x and y.
  2. Calculate the mean of x and y.
  3. The mean of data set is ratio of sum of all data values to the number of data items.
  4. Substitute the values in the formula r = Σ [(xi - xmean)(yi - ymean] / √Σi(xi - xmean)² √Σi(yi - ymean)².
  5. Perform the math operations to get the value of r.

FAQs on Pearson Correlation Coefficient Calculator

1. What is the formula to calculate the Pearson correlation coefficient?

The Pearson correlation coefficient formula is r = [n(∑xy) - (∑x)(∑y)] / √(n∑x² - (∑x)²) (n∑y² - (∑y)².

2. How is Pearson correlation calculated?

Get the sample values of x and y. Find ∑x, ∑y, ∑xy, ∑x² and ∑y² values. Substitute these values in the formula of Pearson Correlation Coefficient and perform calculations to check the correlation coefficient value.

3. What does a Pearson correlation of 0.05 mean?

A value of 0.05 indicates that the risk of concluding that correlation exists when actually, no correlation exists is 5%.

4. What are the types of correlation?

There are 4 types of correlations. They are Pearson correlation, Spearman correlation, Kendall rank correlation and Point Biserial correlation.
