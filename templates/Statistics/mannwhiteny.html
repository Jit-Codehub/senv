What is the Mann-Whitney U test?

The Mann-Whitney U test is a process that can be used when we have two independent samples and want to know if they come from the same distribution or from shifted distributions.

The Mann-Whitney U test is also called as Wilcoxon-Mann-Whitney test as this was first proposed by Wilcoxon and later developed by Mann and Whitney. This process makes use of the test statistic U.

Mann-Whitney U Test nterpretation

The null hypothesis of the Mann-Whitney U test if two populations have the same distribution, then those two populations have equal medians. In the case of rejectance of the null hypothesis means we have evidence that the population distributions are shifted with respect to each other, so are their medians. Three possible alternatives of the distributions are listed here:

  • The first distribution is shifted to the right with respect to the second distribution. So, the median of the first population is greater than the median of the second population.
  • The first distribution is shifted to the right or left with respect to the second distribution. The median of the first population is different from the median of the second population.
  • The first distribution is shifted to the left with respect to the second distribution. The median of the first population is lesser than the median of second population.

Critical values for the Mann-Whitney U test

The critical value depends on the alternative hypothesis.

  • If A ≠ B, then U₁ is unusually small or unusually large. So, the critical region is two-sided i.e (-∞, c₁] ∪ [c₂, ∞) and c₁, c₂ are critical values.
  • If A > B, then sample A observations are greater than sample B observations. Hence, U₁ is unusually large, and so the critical region is right-sided, i.e., [c, ∞).
  • If A < B, then the sample B observations are less than sample A observations. Therefore, U₁ is unusually small, and so the critical region is left-sided, i.e., (∞, c].

FAQs on Mann-Whitney U test Calculator

1. How do you calculate Mann-Whitney U Test?

To find the Mann-Whitney U Test of two populations, enter the population data set, select tail and significance level from the provided input sections and hit the calculate button.

2. What is Mann-Whitney U Test used for?

The Mann-Whitney U Test is used for comparing whether there is a difference in the dependent variable for two independent groups. It also compares whether the distribution of the dependent variable is the same for two groups and for the same population.

3. Is Mann-Whitney U Test paired?

The Mann-Whitney U Test is a non-parametric test that is used in place of an unpaired t-test. It is used to test the null hypothesis that two samples come from the same population.

4. What is the difference between the Mann-Whitney U and Wilcoxon rank-sum tests?

The Mann-Whitney U test and Wilcoxon rank-sum tests are the same. So, the Mann-Whitney U test is also called as Mann-Whitney-Wilcoxon test. Though we can find two different test statistics, it turns out they are closely related. No matter, tests are the same.
