What is meant by Odds?

Odds are mainly calculated in ratio and there are two types of ratios. One is "odds of winning" and the other one is "odds of losing". For example, the odd of winning a lottery might be 50 to 10000. Here, the first number is the chances of success and the second number is chances against success. And for odds of losing it will be viceversa.

Formula of Odds

Probability of winning = Pw = A / (A + B).

probability of Losing = PL = B / (A + B).

Where,

A = Chances of success.

B = Chances against success.

A+B = All chances

Odds Probability Examples

Question 1: Find the odds in favor of a number cube landing on 4?

Solution

First, decide the number that represents the chances of success and chances against success.

Chances of success(A) =1 (4)

Chances against Success(B) = 5(1,2,3,5,6)

Apply the Formula.

All chances = A+B = 1+5 =6

Probability of winning = Chances for success/ All chances.

= A/(A+B) = 1/6 =16.67%

Probability of Loosing = Chances against success / All chances.

= B/(A+B) = 5/6 = 83.33%

Odds Conversions

Fractional odds = 5/1

Decimal odds = 6
