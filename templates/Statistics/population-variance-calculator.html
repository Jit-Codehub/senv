What is Population Variance?

In statistics, population variance is the set of comparable events which is of interest for some experiment or investigation. A statistical population can be a group of existing objects perceived as a generalization from experience.

Variance is the measure of the variability of values in the data set. It is defined as the average squared difference from the mean of all data points. Low variance means the data is more tightly clustered and high variance means the dataset is more spread out.

The symbol used to represent population variance is σ². Its formula is given here:

μ is the mean

σ² is the variance

Step by Step Process to Calculate Population Variance

The following are the detailed steps are helpful to calculate the population variance of data set. Follow them to obtain instant results.

  • Take any data set.
  • Find the difference from the mean of each point.
  • Square the difference from mean for each point.
  • Add the all points squared values.
  • Divide the sum by total number of data points to find population variance.

Example:

Question: Find the Population Variance of data set {5, 6, 8, 3, 10, 7, 13}?

Solution:

Given data set values are {5, 6, 8, 3, 10, 7, 13}

Population variance formula is σ² = ∑(xi - μ)² / N

Mean μ = [5+6+8+3+10+7+13]/7

= 52/7 = 7.428

Substitute the values in the formula of population variance

σ² = [(5-7.428)²+(6-7.428)²+(8-7.428)²+(3-7.428)²+(10-7.428)²+(7-7.428)²+(13-7.428)²]/7

= [5.895+2.039+0.327+19.607+6.615+0.183+31.047]/7

= 65.713/7

= 9.387

Therefore, the population variance of the data is 9.387.

FAQs on Population Variance Calculator

1. How to find population variance in a calculator?

You have to give the data set values in the input section of the calculator and click on the calculate button to avail the exact population variance value with a detailed step by step explanation in the output section.

2. What are the differences between sample variance and population variance?

The population variance is the value of variance that is calculated from the population data. Whereas sample variance is the variance value calculated from the sample data. The population variance formula is σ2 = ∑(xi - μ)2 / N. The sample variance is the replacement of N by N-1 in population variance. So, the formula of sample variance is s2 = ∑(xi - x̄)2 / (N - 1).

3. What is the population variance formula?

The formula to determine population variance is σ2 = ∑ (x − x̅)² / N. Where N is the total number of observations and x̅ is the mean.

4. Why do we calculate population variance?

Population variance is an important measure of dispersion that is used in statistics. We calculate population variance to determine how individuals in the data set relate to each other.
