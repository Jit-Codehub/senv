What is the Population Mean in Statistics?

In statistics, Population Mean is known as an average of a group characteristic. The pool of persons from which a statistical sample is drawn for a study is called a population in statistics. The symbol of the Population Mean is μ.

For instance, In a classroom of 100 students, the average GPA is 2.5.

Population Mean Formula

The formula to calculate the population mean deviation for the given data set is as follows:

Population mean (μ) = Sum of the all Items/Total Number of Items

Population mean (μ) = S/N

where,

μ is the population mean

S is Sum of the elements/items

N is the total number of elements/items

Detailed Steps on Estimation of the Population Mean PDF

The simple steps that you should follow carefully to solve the population mean are listed below:

  • Firstly, memorize the formula of the population mean deviation.
  • Now, calculate the sum of each and every data item.
  • Finally, divide the result by the total number of items to get the population mean value.

Have a look at the below-solved example on how to find the population mean of the given data set and practice well efficiently & clear all your doubts.

Example:

Find the population mean of the following data set: 13, 28, 49, 68, 95

Solution:

Given data set is 13, 28, 49, 68, 95

Now, find the sum of the given data values ie., S = 13+28+49+68+95

S = 253

Later, find the total number of values given ie., N = 5

Apply the population mean formula i.e, (μ) = S/N

μ = 253/5

μ = 50.6

Hence, the population mean of the 13, 28, 49, 68, 95 data set is 50.6

FAQs on Handy Online Population Mean Calculator Statistics

1. How do you calculate the population mean?

In order to calculate the population mean, you have to sum up all the given data values and then divide the result by the total number of values count. Thus, you can get the result of the population mean of the given data set.

2. What does population mean called?

The Population Mean is called the actual mean of the entire population of the given data items.

3. Is the sample mean equal to the population mean?

The sampling distribution of the mean will consistently be the same as the mean of the original non-normal distribution so in short, the sample mean is similar to the population mean.
