What is Relative Standard Deviation?

The relative standard deviation is also called percentage standard deviation, is a special form of standard deviation. The deviation measure gives information about how the different numbers in the specified data set are scattered around the mean.

The formula to find the relative standard deviation is given as:

Relative Standard DEviation, RSD = (Standard Deviation x 100)/Mean

How to Calculate Relative Standard Deviation?

Below given are the simple steps to find the relative standard deviation of the data sample easily.

  • Firstly, find the sum of numbers in the sample.
  • Divide the square of sum of numbers by the total number of items in the sample.
  • Calculate sum of squares of data items in the sample.
  • Divide it by the number of items minus 1 to get the variance.
  • Find the square root of variance to know the standard deviation value.
  • Also, compute the mean of the sample.
  • Multiply the SD with 100 and divide product by mean to find relative standard deviation.

Example:

Question: What is the Relative Standard Deviation of sample {15, 12, 10, 8, 5, 6, 2}?

Solution

Given sample is {15, 12, 10, 8, 5, 6, 2}

Sum of data items in sample=15+12+10+8+5+6+2=58

(square of sum of numbers)/number of data items=58x58/7=480.57

Sum of squares of data items=152+122+102+82+52+62+22=225+144+100+64+25+36+4=568

568-480.57=87.43

Variance=87.43/(7-1)=14.571

Standard Deviation=sqrt(variance)=sqrt(14.571)=3.817

Relative Standard Deviation=SD*100/mean

Mean=Sum of data items/ number of data item

=58/7=8.285

RSD=sqrt(14.571)*100/8.285=46.07

Relative Standard Deviation is 46.07%

FAQs on Relative Standard Deviation Calculator

1. How do you calculate relative standard deviation?

At first, you need to find the mean and standard deviation of the sample. Multiply the standard deviation with 100 and divide it by mean to get the relative standard deviation value.

2. What is Meant by Relative Standard Deviation?

The relative standard deviation RSD is a special form of standard deviation, where it is presented as a percentage of the mean. It is always positive. The common way to write RSD after mean is with a plus or minus sign.

3. What is the relation between standard deviation and relative standard deviation?

A relative standard deviation is a form of standard deviation that is calculated by multiplying SD with 100 and dividing by the mean value.

4. What is the RSD formula?

The formula to calculate relative standard deviation is RSD = (Standard deviation x 100)/Mean.
